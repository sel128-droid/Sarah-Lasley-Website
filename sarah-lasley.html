About

Sarah Lasley — Chicago, IL

I make films that live in the uncomfortable middle — between beauty and mess, between documentary truth and cinematic artifice.

Based in Chicago, I've spent the last decade behind the camera and occasionally in front of a monitor yelling (quietly, professionally) about lens choices. My work spans narrative features, documentary, short film, and the occasional music video that I'll describe as "an experiment" and my mother will describe as "confusing."

I trained at Columbia College Chicago and have since shot on four continents, two film stocks that no longer exist, and one borrowed camera that I will never talk about publicly. My cinematography foregrounds texture, shadow, and the kind of light that makes people look like they have something to hide.

When I'm not on set, I'm probably rewatching something by Agnès Varda or arguing about aspect ratios at dinner. Both are productive activities.
